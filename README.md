# Exploratory-Data-Analysis
Exploratory Data Analysis for Time Series Analysis.
Assignment description:
- In the EDA, investigate the data and evaluate its temporal features. Undertaking data cleaning, visualisation, and statistical tests in a coherent workflow. The EDA must be clearly written and well structured but does not require formal presentation as a report.
- Include your GitHub address in the EDA report, so that I can check your GitHub repository. You need to reposite your preliminary work regularly in the repository, so that I can track your sequences of steps leading to your final models.
- The data is the Singapore birth and fertilityLinks to an external site. data. Also look hereLinks to an external site. for more information.
- You are only required to look at Total Live Births (TLB) and Total Fertility Rate (TFR) from 1960 to 2024.
- For EDA, you can take the entire 1960 to 2024 data for TLB and TFR, and produce plots, comment on the data etc.
- Eventually for the Final Report, you should come up with at least 2 viable time series models each for TLB and TFR for the period from 1960 to 2012, and use your models to "forecast" the TLB and TFR from 2013 to 2024, but for the EDA, you should have at least 1 potential time series model for the TLB and for the TFR for the period 1960 to 2012.
- For the EDA, you should also discuss how you can assess which of your viable models is best for forecasting for the period from 2013 to 2024.
- If you have literature references for time series modelling of TLB and TFR (whether in the Singapore context or other countries), include a short discussion on the literature, since the techniques in the literature may be relevant.
- There could be social and economic issues that influence your decision to choose the final set of models to eventually include in your final report, rather than what statistical test and standard time series analysis methodology indicate. In this case, be prepared to justify your choice in the Final Report.
